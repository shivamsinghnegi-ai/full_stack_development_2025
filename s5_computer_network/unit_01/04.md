# Types of Network, Internet, Intranet, Extranet

---

## 1. Types of Network

Networks are classified based on their **geographical coverage/scale**.

| Type | Full Form | Coverage Area | Speed | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PAN | Personal Area Network | A few meters (around one person) | High (short range) | Bluetooth connection, USB devices |
| LAN | Local Area Network | Single building/campus (up to a few km) | Very high | Office network, college computer lab |
| MAN | Metropolitan Area Network | A city (up to ~50 km) | High | Cable TV network, city-wide Wi-Fi |
| WAN | Wide Area Network | Country/Continent/Global | Comparatively lower | The Internet, bank ATM networks |

### LAN (Local Area Network)
- Covers a small geographical area (office, school, single building)
- Owned by a single organization
- High data transfer rate, low error rate
- Uses Ethernet cables or Wi-Fi

### MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
- Covers a larger area than LAN (a city or town)
- Often used to connect several LANs together
- Example: Cable TV networks, government city networks

### WAN (Wide Area Network)
- Covers a very large geographical area (country or globally)
- Made up of multiple interconnected LANs and MANs
- Uses public or leased communication lines (satellite, fiber optics)
- Example: The Internet itself is the largest WAN

**Exam Point:** LAN vs MAN vs WAN comparison (based on area, speed, ownership, cost) is a very common exam question.

## 2. Internet

### Definition
The **Internet** is a global system of interconnected computer networks that uses the standard **TCP/IP protocol suite** to connect billions of devices worldwide.

### Key Characteristics
- Publicly accessible (open to everyone)
- No single owner; it is a network of networks
- Uses TCP/IP for communication
- Provides services like WWW, email, file transfer, video streaming

### Uses of Internet
1. Communication (email, chat, video calls)
2. Information access (search engines, research)
3. E-commerce and online banking
4. Entertainment (streaming, gaming)
5. Cloud storage and computing

---

## 3. Intranet

### Definition
An **Intranet** is a private network that uses Internet technologies (TCP/IP, web browsers) but is restricted to a single organization. It is accessible only to employees or authorized members within that organization.

### Key Characteristics
- Private and restricted access (requires login/authentication)
- Protected by firewalls from the public Internet
- Used for internal communication, sharing company data, policies, and resources

### Uses of Intranet
1. Internal communication (company announcements, emails)
2. Sharing internal documents and resources
3. Employee collaboration tools
4. Internal databases and knowledge sharing

**Exam Point:** Intranet = "Internet technology, but private to one organization."

---

## 4. Extranet

### Definition
An **Extranet** is an extension of an Intranet that allows **controlled access to external users**, such as business partners, suppliers, or customers, in addition to internal employees.

### Key Characteristics
- Semi-private network (partially open to outsiders)
- Requires authentication/authorization for external users
- Used for business-to-business (B2B) collaboration

### Uses of Extranet
1. Sharing data with business partners and suppliers
2. Allowing customers to track orders or access support
3. Collaborative projects between different organizations

---

## Comparison: Internet vs Intranet vs Extranet

| Basis | Internet | Intranet | Extranet |
|---|---|---|---|
| Access | Public (everyone) | Private (organization only) | Semi-private (organization + selected outsiders) |
| Ownership | No single owner | Owned by one organization | Owned by one organization, shared access |
| Security | Least secure | Highly secure | Moderately secure |
| Purpose | Global information sharing | Internal communication | External collaboration (B2B) |
| Example | Websites, search engines | Company employee portal | Supplier/partner portal |

**Exam Point:** This comparison table (Internet vs Intranet vs Extranet) is one of the most frequently asked questions in this unit — memorize the access/security/purpose differences.

---

## Summary

1. **Types of Network by scale** → PAN, LAN, MAN, WAN.
2. **LAN** → small area, high speed, single organization.
3. **MAN** → city-wide, connects multiple LANs.
4. **WAN** → country/global, Internet is the largest WAN.
5. **Internet** → global, public, uses TCP/IP.
6. **Intranet** → private, restricted to one organization.
7. **Extranet** → private + limited external access for partners/suppliers.
