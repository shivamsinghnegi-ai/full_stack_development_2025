# Soft Skills Activities

## 1. Internship Interview Role-Play

Skill Focus: Confidence, interview readiness, communication

### Setup

* Divide students into groups of 3
* Each group assigns roles:

  * Interviewer: asks questions and maintains a professional tone
  * Candidate: answers questions as if attending a real internship interview
  * Observer: silently watches and evaluates

### Situation

Students imagine they are applying for their first internship at a company. This is their initial screening interview.

### Interview Questions

The interviewer asks the candidate:

1. Tell me about yourself  
2. What skills do you have?  
3. Why should we select you for this internship?  

### Observer Responsibilities

The observer focuses on:

* Confidence (voice clarity, calmness)  
* Clear and structured answers  
* Body language (eye contact, posture)  

### Flow

* Interview runs for 5–7 minutes  
* Observer gives short feedback  
* Roles are rotated so every student experiences each role  

### Learning Outcome

Students understand how interviews feel, improve confidence, and learn how communication and body language affect interview performance.

## 2. Startup Team Simulation

Skill Focus: Leadership, teamwork, decision-making

### Setup

* Divide students into groups of 4–5  
* Each group acts as a startup team  
* Assign roles within the group:

  * Team Lead  
  * Developer  
  * Marketing  
  * Finance  
  * HR (optional)

### Situation

Students imagine they are final-year college students who decide to start a startup instead of sitting for placements.

### Task Details

Each group must decide:

1. Product idea  
2. Role responsibilities  
3. First priority action  

### Rules

* Total time: 8 minutes  
* Every member must speak at least once  

### Learning Outcome

Students experience leadership dynamics and group decision-making.

## 3. Continue the Story (Group Chain)

Skill Focus: Communication, creativity, listening

### Setup

* Groups of 4–5 students  
* Same story starter for all  

### Story Starter

"It was the first day of internship, and the system suddenly crashed..."

### Rules

* Each student speaks for 30 seconds  
* No interruptions  

### Learning Outcome

Improves listening, thinking speed, and expression.

## 4. Pair Role-Play: Explain to a Non-Tech Person

Skill Focus: Communication, clarity

### Setup

* Students in pairs  

### Task Topics

* What is the internet?  
* What is an app?  
* What is online payment?  

### Rules

* 3 minutes each  
* No technical words  

### Learning Outcome

Students learn simple and clear communication.

## 8. Role Play Discussion (Multiple Perspectives)

Skill Focus: Real-life communication, persuasion, perspective thinking

### Setup

* Groups of 4 students  
* Assign roles:

  * CEO  
  * Employee  
  * Customer  
  * Government  

### Topic

"A company is replacing workers with automation"

### Flow

* Each student speaks from their role’s point of view  
* Group discusses possible outcomes  

### Learning Outcome

Students improve real-world communication, persuasive speaking, and understanding different viewpoints.

## 9. Case Study Discussion

Skill Focus: Logical thinking, teamwork, problem-solving

### Situation

"A college fest budget is limited. How should money be spent?"

### Setup

* Groups of 4 students  

### Task

* Discuss possible spending options  
* Agree on a final plan together  

### Rules

* Everyone must speak at least once  
* One final decision required  

### Learning Outcome

Students practice structured thinking, collaboration, and solution-focused communication.
