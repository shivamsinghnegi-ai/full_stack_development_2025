# Soft Skills Classroom Activities

## 1. Startup Team Simulation

Skill Focus: Leadership, teamwork, decision-making

### Setup

* Divide students into groups of 4–5
* Each group represents one startup team

### Scenario

Students are final-year college students who decide to start a startup together.

### Roles (assigned within the group)

* Team Lead
* Developer
* Marketing
* Finance
* HR 

### Task

Each group must collectively decide:

1. Product idea (what problem they are solving)
2. Role responsibilities (who does what)
3. First priority (most important action to take first)

### Rules

* Total time: 8 minutes
* Every member must speak at least once
* Decisions must be made as a group

### Learning Outcome

Students understand how different roles think, how leadership works in teams, and how to make decisions under time pressure.

## 2. Internship Interview Role-Play

Skill Focus: Confidence, interview readiness, communication

### Setup

* Divide students into groups of 3
* Each group assigns the following roles:

  * Interviewer
  * Candidate
  * Observer

### Interview Questions

The interviewer asks the candidate:

1. Tell me about yourself
2. What skills do you have?
3. Why should we select you for this internship?

### Observer Responsibilities

The observer silently evaluates the candidate based on:

* Confidence while speaking
* Clarity of answers
* Body language (eye contact, posture, calmness)

### Learning Outcome

Students practice real interview situations, build confidence, and understand how communication and body language impact interviews.

## 3. Continue the Story (Group Chain)

Skill Focus: Communication, creativity, listening

### Setup

* Divide students into groups of 4–5
* Provide one common starting story to all groups

### Story Starter

"It was the first day of internship, and the system suddenly crashed..."

### Rules

* Student 1 speaks for 2min
* Student 2 continues the story for the next 2min
* Each student must continue from the previous part

### Flow

* Continue until all students in the group have contributed
* Groups may end the story naturally or stop when time is up

### Learning Outcome

Students practice active listening, clear communication, and creative thinking while building on others' ideas.
